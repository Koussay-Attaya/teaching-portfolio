# Teaching Philosophy

I believe that students learn best when they actively participate in the learning process.

Rather than simply explaining concepts, I encourage students to build, test, experiment, and improve their own solutions. My classroom combines engineering design, technology, creativity, and collaboration to help students develop confidence through practical experience.

Every lesson is designed to answer one simple question:

**"How can students learn by doing?"**

---

# Educational Approach

My teaching approach is built around five key principles:

### Project-Based Learning

Students learn through designing and building real projects rather than memorizing information.

### Engineering Design Process

Students identify problems, brainstorm solutions, build prototypes, test their work, and refine their designs.

### Hands-On Learning

Every lesson includes practical activities where students assemble circuits, program Arduino boards, and test hardware.

### Collaborative Learning

Students work together, exchange ideas, and develop teamwork and communication skills.

### Reflective Learning

Students evaluate their work, identify improvements, and learn from both successes and mistakes.
